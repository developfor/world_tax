Global Taxes for Medium Sized Companies

The Doing Business Report is a study from the World Bank Group. The data for these visualisations is from Paying Taxes and looks a the taxes and mandatory contributions a medium-sized company but pay or withhold in a given year.

This visualisation looks at 7 regions for the following tax infomation:

  • Payments - number of payments per year
  • Time - number of hours per year spent completing taxes for the company
  • Total Tax Rate - % of profit

OECD is the Organistion for Economic Co-operation and Development and is a group of 34 countries. The Doing Business Fact Sheet lists 21 of these contries as High-Income Economies. According to the Doing Business factsheet the OECD High-Income Economies in these visualisations are:

Australia, Austria, Belgium, Canada, Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Iceland, Ireland, Israel, Italy, Japan, Republic of Korea, Luxembourg, The Netherlands, New Zealand, Norway, Poland, Portugal, Slovak Republic, Slovenia, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, United Kingdom, United States.
